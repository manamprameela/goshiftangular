Why Shift?

Shift work is common for almost any business that operates for 8 hours a day or longer. The reason for this is that employers don't have the financial resources or they're unwilling to pay employees overtime. As a result, they'll split the workload into shifts that keep their workers below 40 hours a week. This reduces overhead, as well as keeping employees alert and sharp.

Convenience

Employees are able to choose the shift according to their needs. A person who needs to take care of a home can opt for night shift and a person who is free in the morning can opt for morning shifts. At the same time, it is beneficial when it comes to taking leave.

Good Work-Life Balance

A healthy balance might look like meeting your deadlines at work while still having time for friends and hobbies. Having enough time to sleep properly and eat well not worrying about work when you're at home.

Health

Taking care of your employees' health in the workplace is not only beneficial to them; it also creates an effective and efficient working environment that benefits the business. Employee health and productivity go hand in hand: A mentally and physically healthy individual is more optimistic, creative, and motivated.
